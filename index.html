Seamless AI Support for CTOUCH Displays

Designing a non-intrusive AI assistant for CTOUCH displays to enhance user interaction with intuitive, seamless support.


About CTOUCH

CTOUCH is a forward-thinking company dedicated to creating dynamic, large-format touch displays. Their mission is to enhance interactivity, productivity, and engagement in both classrooms and business environments by leveraging the potential of touch technology. The company stands out for its innovative vision and user-centric approach to collaboration, offering tailored solutions that elevate the touch experience.





Design Challenge


Problem Statement:

Current support tools often feel either intrusive (like Clippy from Windows) or embarrassing when users require assistance in a public setting, such as standing in front of a class or during a meeting. The challenge is to design an AI assistant that seamlessly integrates into the existing support tool, offering intuitive help without disrupting the flow of interaction.


Objective:

Create an AI assistant that:

  • Feels like a natural, human-like assistant, not a robotic tool.
  • Enhances user friendliness by providing non-intrusive, context-aware support.
  • Works across multiple use cases, such as teaching, presentations, and meetings.




Methodology


Phase 1: Research

  • User Interviews: Conduct interviews with target users, including teachers, students, and business professionals, to understand their needs, frustrations, and preferences.
  • AI Capabilities Research: Study the current state of AI in interaction design to understand available functionalities and limitations.
  • Competitive Analysis: Analyze existing touchscreen displays with built-in AI support to identify gaps and opportunities for differentiation.


Phase 2: Ideation

  • Could-have: Optional features that can further improve engagement.

Sketching Concepts:

Develop initial design ideas through sketches, focusing on user-friendly interactions and intuitive AI responses.


Phase 3: Prototyping


Figma Prototypes:

Build high-fidelity prototypes in Figma to visualize the AI assistantâ€™s interface and behavior.


User Testing:

Conduct usability tests with real users to gather feedback and iterate on the design.

A User-Centered App for Managing Sensory Overload

OFF is a smart mobile app designed to help users manage sensory overload through personalized calming features, intuitive navigation, and seamless interaction. Developed using an iterative, user-centered approach, the app ensures a smooth and stress-free experience tailored to individual needs.


Objective:

Develop a smart mobile app that helps users manage sensory overload by providing calming, personalized support through an intuitive and user-friendly design.





Key Features of the App:


  • Personalized Calming Audio:
    Users can select and customize calming audio tracks based on their preferences to help reduce sensory overload in real time.
  • Planning & Checklist:
    Users can create personalized daily plans with reminders for important tasks, while the checklist feature allows them to write down what they need to take with them or note essential items for each plan, helping them stay organized and prepared.
  • Progress Tracking Diagram:
    Visual progress tracking helps users become aware of their achievements over time. The diagram shows completed tasks, frequency of sensory overload, and coping success, empowering users with insights into their improvement.
  • Interactive Home Screen:
    Displays real-time weather updates and personalized plans for the day, ensuring a calming and organized user experience.
  • Seamless Navigation:
    Implemented a bottom navigation bar for easy access to core features, enhancing usability and reducing cognitive load.
  • User Profile & Account Management:
    Secure login and account creation features ensure user data privacy while allowing personalized experiences.

  • Used the DOT-framework to define key user needs and market trends.
  • Ideated and refined mobile concepts through structured feedback and testing.

Design Execution:

  • Created key screens (Login, Create Account, Home, New Plan) with intuitive navigation and consistent styling.
  • Redesigned elements based on usability feedback to improve user interaction and clarity.

Development & Realization:

  • Developed core app functionalities using Flutter, ensuring secure authentication and smooth performance.
  • Integrated backend services and hardware sensors for enhanced functionality.

Advisory Contributions:

  • Recommended design enhancements (e.g., bottom navigation bar) based on user testing and cognitive walkthroughs.
  • Provided actionable advice on improving navigation and overall user experience.

Project Management:

  • Maintained effective communication with stakeholders through Slack and Jira.
  • Ensured project alignment and timely completion using industry-standard management techniques.

Core Skills Demonstrated:

  • User-Centered Design & Usability Testing
  • UI/UX Prototyping & Iterative Development
  • Mobile Development (Flutter, Kotlin)
  • Stakeholder Communication & Project Management

Impact:

The app delivers a personalized, user-friendly solution for individuals managing sensory overload, with features designed to minimize stress through thoughtful interaction design. Rigorous usability testing ensured an intuitive experience that meets real-world needs.

3D Modeling Portfolio: From Architectural Visualization to Character Design

In the evolving landscape of 3D modeling and visualization, the ability to navigate between different styles and requirements is crucial. This portfolio showcases two distinct projects that demonstrate versatility in 3D modeling, from grand architectural visualization to detailed character design. Each project presented unique challenges and learning opportunities, contributing to a comprehensive skill set in 3D artistry.


Project 1: Wallace's Archive - Blade Runner 2049 Recreation


Inspired by the iconic science fiction film Blade Runner 2049, this project challenged the boundaries between architectural visualization and cinematic atmosphere. The recreation of Wallace's Archive demanded not just technical precision in modeling, but an understanding of how architecture and lighting work together to create mood and atmosphere.

The development process included:

  • Detailed architectural modeling with precise attention to structural elements
  • Complex material creation and shading to capture the unique aesthetic
  • Strategic lighting setup to recreate the film's distinctive atmosphere
  • High-resolution rendering with emphasis on reflective surfaces and shadows


The final result captures the imposing grandeur of the original scene while demonstrating advanced skills in architectural visualization and cinematic lighting.




Project 2: Interactive Rubber Duck - Character Modeling


This project explored the nuances of character modeling through the creation of a seemingly simple but technically challenging rubber duck model. The goal was to create a high-quality asset that could seamlessly integrate into Unity, requiring careful attention to optimization and detail.

The development process included:

  • Precise modeling and sculpting of organic shapes
  • Strategic UV layout for efficient texturing
  • Custom texture creation for characteristic rubber material
  • Rigorous model cleanup and optimization for game engine compatibility


Despite its playful appearance, this project demanded sophisticated technical skills and a methodical approach to 3D modeling and asset preparation.
